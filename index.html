Less Prisons, More Mercy

Mission

The mission of Less Prisons, More Mercy is to educate and raise awareness about mass incarceration and its association with poverty, colored communities, and mentally ill or disabled people. Our goal is to ultimately reform the criminal justice system to focus less on punishment and more on rehabilitation and healing.


Don't hate, rehabilitate. Let's have mercy on everyone, equally.

Statistics

Black men are six times as likely to be incarcerated as white men and Latinos are 2.5 times as likely. For Black men in their thirties, about 1 in every 12 is in prison or jail on any given day.

There are 2 million people in the nation’s prisons and jails—a 500% increase over the last 40 years. Changes in sentencing law and policy, not changes in crime rates, explain most of this increase. These trends have resulted in prison overcrowding and fiscal burdens on states to accommodate a rapidly expanding penal system, despite increasing evidence that large-scale incarceration is not an effective means of achieving public safety.
